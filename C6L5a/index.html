Parallax scrolling is a web design technique in which the background content (i.e., an image) is moved at a different speed than the foreground content while scrolling. This effect is widely used to create a sense of depth and engagement on web pages. By employing parallax scrolling, designers can make websites more dynamic and visually appealing, often resulting in a better user experience.

The parallax effect can be achieved through various methods, including CSS, JavaScript, and HTML5. It is important to use this technique judiciously, as overuse can lead to performance issues, especially on mobile devices. Well-implemented parallax scrolling can enhance the storytelling aspect of a website, guiding users through the content in a more interactive and engaging manner.
